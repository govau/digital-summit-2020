Day 1: Disruption and Change

Recent crises have seen government and industry accelerate digital transformation, crucial to business continuity and rapid disaster response.

Thought leaders spoke on digital transformation through crisis, the opportunities change presents, and how we adapt to change and work through disruption:

  • innovation and entrepreneurship
  • digital disruption
  • recent crises such as the Australian bushfires and COVID-19
  • digital transformation.

You can:

  • Understand and capitalise on the digital transformation opportunities change creates.
  • Get inspiration and hear from experts who have innovated through change.
  • Learn how to lead and manage through change and disruption.
  • Learn practical applications of healthy and productive disruption.

Day 2: Human-centred Services

Driving a human-centred approach to services should be at the heart of everything we do. More agencies are prioritising new tactics to design for and with users, employees, and industry partners.

Advocates in user experience design shared inspiration and strategies for understanding and meeting user needs, including:

  • service and experience design
  • human-centred design
  • government as a service.

You can:

  • Learn how to understand and meet user needs and design better services.
  • Learn how co-design can help government transform the way it delivers products and services to meet the changing needs of citizens.
  • Learn how to use design to create powerful solutions to contemporary problems.

Day 4: Technology and Data

We need technology and data to improve the user experience of government services for citizens and businesses. Understanding and interpreting well data delivers better outcomes.

Experts in digital security and privacy provided their insights and experience. They showed us how to use data in an ethical and socially responsible way:

  • cyber security
  • artificial intelligence and machine learning
  • data privacy, ethics, and social licence
  • platforms as a service.

You can:

  • Learn how data can help you understand users and lead to greater insights.
  • Discover and explore the ethical boundaries of the opportunities data creates.
  • Understand how new technology, platforms and approaches will continue to create new opportunities for agencies and digital practitioners.
