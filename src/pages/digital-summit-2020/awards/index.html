Australian government digital awards 2020 - award criteria

Recognising and celebrating the best in digital practice

The Australian Government Digital Awards recognise the best examples of digital practice across government and industry working with government.

Awards

This year we're awarding:

  1. Outstanding digital product or service
  2. Outstanding government platform
  3. Outstanding use of emerging technology
  4. Outstanding digital methodology or approach
  5. Excellence in government/industry partnership
  6. Excellence in multi-agency partnership
  7. Emerging digital talent (individual with less than 5 years in digital role)
  8. Digital professional (individual with more than 5 years in digital role).

Finalists will prepare a short video to show as part of the award proceedings.

This year the Awards are part of the Digital Summit on 10, 12, and 17 November 2020.

1. Outstanding digital product or service

The winner of this category has created an innovative product or service that demonstrably improves the end-user experience. The process for developing the product or service was user-focused and followed appropriate government and industry standards and protocols.

Category criteria

The criteria for this category are:

  1. user-centred design
  2. innovative use of technology
  3. full compliance with accessibility requirements, including WCAG 2.0.

General criteria

The general criteria for this category are:

  1. demonstration of tangible benefits to users
  2. adoption of industry and government standards and protocols.

2. Outstanding government platform

The winner of this category has developed a platform that makes it easier to deliver agency services and for users to access those services. Ideally the platform helped remove duplication and break down silos. The process for developing the platform was user-focussed and followed appropriate government and industry standards and protocols.

Category criteria

The criteria for this category are:

  1. openness and/or co-production of platform design *
  2. richness of platform ecosystem
  3. reusability of platform for multiple purposes and/or agencies.

General criteria

The general criteria for this category are:

  1. demonstration of tangible benefits to users
  2. adoption of industry and government standards and protocols, including alignment to the Digital Service Platforms Strategy where applicable.

* Privacy or security sensitivities can limit the openness of some platforms. Recipients must have demonstrated engagement with others and the other methods used to empower outside contributors or influencers.

3. Outstanding use of emerging technology

The winner of this category has created an innovative product or service using emerging technology to demonstrably improve the delivery of the product or service. The process for developing the product or service followed appropriate government and industry standards and protocols.

Category criteria

The emerging technology:

  1. enabled the product or service to better meet user needs
  2. was the solution that was the best fit for the problem
  3. was reusable.

General criteria

The general criteria for this category are:

  1. demonstration of tangible benefits to users
  2. adoption of industry and government standards and protocols.

4. Outstanding digital methodology or approach

The winner of this category was a product or service that went through an innovative process and was designed in partnership with stakeholders and the end user. The process for developing the product or service followed appropriate government and industry standards and protocols.

Category criteria

The criteria for this category are:

  1. use of innovative methodology throughout the process of designing and delivering the product or service
  2. reusability of the methodology.

General criteria

The general criteria for this category are:

  1. demonstration of tangible benefits to users
  2. adoption of industry and government standards and protocols.

5. Excellence in government/industry partnership

The partnership should show:

  • an understanding of the user needs and why the partnership was formed
  • a focus on design, development and delivery of the product or service
  • how government delivered a more effective product or service through collaboration with industry.

Category criteria

The criteria for this category are:

  1. ability of partnership to address user needs
  2. delivery of outcomes through the partnership
  3. integration of partners to deliver outcomes.

General criteria

The general criteria for this category are:

  1. demonstration of tangible benefits to users
  2. adoption of industry and government standards and protocols.

7. Emerging digital talent

The winner of this award was a government employee in a digital role for 5 years or less.

The winner followed the appropriate government or industry standards in their work and showed a commitment to digital transformation. They performed their role as a member of a multi-disciplinary team with excellence as recognised by their peers. They also demonstrated initiative beyond their defined role.

Entry criteria

The criteria for this category are:

  1. 5 years or less experience in a digital role in government
  2. nomination by a colleague or sponsor
  3. 2 supporting referees.

Category criteria

The general criteria for this category are:

  1. a commitment to digital transformation
  2. demonstration of initiative beyond their defined role
  3. contribution to digital development of industry and government.

8. Digital professional

The winner of this award will be a government employee with more than 5 years of experience in a digital role.

The winner followed appropriate government or industry standards in their work and showed a commitment to digital transformation. They performed their role as a member of a multi-disciplinary team with excellence as recognised by their peers. They also showed leadership within and beyond their team.

Entry criteria

The criteria for this category are:

  1. more than 5 years' experience in a digital role in government
  2. nomination by a colleague or sponsor
  3. 2 supporting referees.

Category criteria

The general criteria for this category are:

  1. a commitment to digital transformation
  2. digital leadership within their agency
  3. contribution to digital development of industry and government.
