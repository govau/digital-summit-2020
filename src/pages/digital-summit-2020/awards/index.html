Australian government digital awards 2020 - award criteria

Recognising and celebrating the best in digital practice

The Australian Government Digital Awards recognise the best examples of digital practice across government and industry working with government.

Awards

This year we're presenting the following awards:

  1. outstanding digital product or service
  2. outstanding government platform
  3. outstanding use of emerging technology
  4. outstanding digital methodology or approach
  5. excellence in government/industry partnership
  6. excellence in multi-agency partnership
  7. emerging digital talent (individual with less than 5 years in digital role)
  8. digital professional (individual with more than 5 years in digital role).

Finalists will prepare a short video to show as part of the award proceedings.

This year the Awards are part of the Digital Summit. They will take place on 10, 12, and 17 November 2020.

1. Outstanding digital product or service

The winner of this category will have created an innovative product or service that demonstrably improves the end user experience. The process for developing the product or service will have been user-focused and will have followed appropriate government and industry standards and protocols.

Category criteria

  1. user-centred design
  2. innovative use of technology
  3. fully compliant with accessibility requirements (including WCAG 2.0).

General criteria

  1. demonstrated tangible benefits to users
  2. adopted industry and government standards and protocols.

2. Outstanding government platform

The winner of this category will have developed a platform that makes it easier to deliver agency services and easier for users to access those services. Ideally the platform will have helped remove duplication and break down silos. The process for developing the platform will have been user-focussed and followed appropriate government and industry standards and protocols.

Category criteria

  1. openness and/or co-production of platform design *
  2. richness of platform ecosystem
  3. reusability of platform for multiple purposes and/or agencies.

General criteria

  1. demonstrated tangible benefits to users
  2. adopted industry and government standards and protocols (including alignment to the Whole of Government Digital Services Platform Strategy where applicable).

* We note that privacy or security sensitivities can limit the openness of some platforms. In that case, please demonstrate how you have engaged with others and what other methods have you used to empower outside contributors/influencers.

3. Outstanding use of emerging technology

The winner of this category will have created an innovative product or service that has used emerging technology to demonstrably improve the delivery of the product or service. The process for developing the product or service will have followed appropriate government and industry standards and protocols.

Category criteria

  1. the emerging technology enabled the product or service to better meet user needs
  2. the emerging technology was the solution that was the best fit for the problem
  3. reusability of the emerging technology.

General criteria

  1. demonstrated tangible benefits to users
  2. adopted industry and government standards and protocols.
{{> back-to-top}}

4. Outstanding digital methodology or approach

The winner of this category will be a product or service that has gone through an innovative process and has been designed in partnership with stakeholders and the end user. The process for developing the product or service will have followed appropriate government and industry standards and protocols.

Category criteria

  1. use of innovative methodology throughout the process of designing and delivering the product or service
  2. reusability of the methodology.

General criteria

  1. demonstrated tangible benefits to users
  2. adopted industry and government standards and protocols.

5. Excellence in government/industry partnership

The partnership should show:

  • an understanding of the user needs and why the partnership was formed
  • a focus on design, development and delivery of the product or service
  • how government delivered a more effective product or service through collaboration with industry.

Category criteria

  1. ability of partnership to address user needs
  2. outcome delivered through the partnership
  3. integration of partners to deliver outcomes.

General criteria

  1. demonstrated tangible benefits to users
  2. adopted industry and government standards and protocols.
{{> back-to-top}}

6. Excellence in multi-agency partnership

The winner of this category will be a government multi-agency partnership (includes federal, state, local and international) that demonstrates:

  • an understanding of the user needs and why the partnership was formed
  • a focus on design, development and delivery of the product or service
  • how government delivered a more effective product or service, through multi-agency collaboration.

Category criteria

  1. ability of partnership to address user needs
  2. outcome delivered through the partnership
  3. integration of partners to deliver outcomes.

General criteria

  1. demonstrated tangible benefits to users
  2. adopted industry and government standards and protocols.
{{> back-to-top}}

7. Emerging digital talent

The winner of this award will be a government employee who has been in a digital role for 5 years or less.

The winner will be someone who follows the appropriate government/industry standards in their work and shows a commitment to digital transformation. They perform their role as a member of a multi-disciplinary team with excellence as recognised by their peers. They also demonstrate initiative beyond their defined role.

Entry criteria

  1. 5 years or less experience in a digital role in government
  2. nominated by a colleague or sponsor
  3. 2 supporting referees.

Category criteria

  1. commitment to digital transformation
  2. demonstrates initiative beyond their defined role
  3. contribution to digital development of industry and government.
{{> back-to-top}}

8. Digital professional

The winner of this award will be a government employee with more than 5 years of experience in a digital role.

The winner will be someone who follows appropriate government or industry standards in their work and shows a commitment to digital transformation. They perform their role as a member of a multi-disciplinary team with excellence as recognised by their peers. They also show leadership within and beyond their team.

Entry criteria

  1. more than 5 years' experience in a digital role in government
  2. nominated by a colleague or sponsor
  3. 2 supporting referees.

Category criteria

  1. commitment to digital transformation
  2. digital leadership within their agency
  3. contribution to digital development of industry and government.
