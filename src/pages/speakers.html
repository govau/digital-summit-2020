Day 1: Disruption and Change

Tuesday 10 November 2020

Recent crises have seen government and industry accelerate digital transformation across departments, agencies and organisations. Digital transformation is crucial to business continuity and rapid response to disasters.

We'll hear from thought leaders on the importance of digital transformation through crisis, the opportunities presented from change, and how we have adapted and worked through change and disruption.

Key topics:

  • digital disruption
  • digital transformation
  • recent crises of the Australian bushfires and COVID-19

Day 2: Human-centred Services

Thursday 12 November 2020

A human-centred approach to services is the focus of everything we do. More agencies are prioritising new ways to design for and with users, employees, and industry partners.

We will hear more about user experience, shared inspiration, and strategies for understanding user needs. We will get insights from stories, experiments, and discoveries, through agile initiatives and new methodologies.

Key topics:

  • service and experience design
  • human-centred design
  • government as a service

Day 3: Leadership and People

Tuesday 12 November 2020

Leadership and people play an essential role in an organisation’s digital transformation journey. Strong and clear digital leadership can transform the way government provides services to users. This includes being ethical and accountable when making workplace decisions, to making sure that the delivery of service meets the needs of a diverse group of users.

We will hear from current and emerging leaders who will share their stories and ways to inspire others on the journey.

Key topics:

  • open leadership
  • ethical leadership and transparent decision-making
  • the future of work
  • workplace innovation
  • agile leadership in government
{{> back-to-top}}

Day 4: Technology and Data

Thursday 19 November 2020

Technology and data are central to improving the user experience for users and businesses when interacting with government. Making sense of data for insights will help deliver better outcomes and services for the digital age.

We will hear from experts in digital security and privacy, and how to use data in an ethical and socially responsible way.

Key topics:

  • utilising technology to leverage success
  • cyber security
  • artificial intelligence and machine learning
  • data privacy, ethics and social license
  • platform as a service
