Deb Anton

Case study

Interim National Data Commissioner, Office of the National Data Commissioner, Department of Prime Minister and Cabinet (PM&C)

Deb was appointed the Interim National Data Commissioner on 9 August 2018. In this role, she has lead the development of reforms to improve the use of government data to deliver public benefit. This has culminated in the Data Availability and Transparency Bill, which aims to modernise government data sharing to improve government service delivery, inform government policies and programs and enable greater research and development. Over the past two decades, she has gravitated to roles that sit at the intersection of technology and policy. Her experience stretches from the early days of broadband policy and programs, to cybersecurity and championing internal transformation through smart use of technology. She has a wealth of experience in the public sector where she has held senior management positions across the Industry, Education and the Attorney-General's portfolios. She holds a Bachelor of Science with Honours, and a Master of Management, both from the Australian National University. She has also completed the Company Directors Course through the Australian Institute of Company Directors.

How the new Data Availability and Transparency legislation will drive innovation, growth and social wellbeing

Government data is an increasingly valuable asset, but its value is not always realised. In some cases, legislative barriers prevent data from being shared. In others, cultural risk aversion leads the custodians of data to avoid sharing it, even if it is lawful, safe and worthwhile to do so.

The Office of the National Data Commissioner has spent the past two years in consultation with data stakeholders from government, private and research communities to develop a new pathway to safely share data with the right people, for the right reasons, and with strong safeguards in place.

The Data Availability and Transparency Bill creates a streamlined pathway for Australian Government agencies to share their data, so that it can be used to improve government service delivery, inform the development of government policies and programs, and facilitate more effective research and development.

Its release in September 2020 marks a significant milestone in the Australian Government's commitment to making better use of the data it already holds and to support its vision for delivering a seamless digital experience for the Australian public. Data is also one of the pillars of the Digital Transformation Strategy released by the Digital Transformation Agency.

This streamlined data sharing scheme will only work if entities sharing and accessing Australian Government data have the skills, capabilities and confidence to share and use data. The Office of the National Data Commissioner is also working to lift data capabilities across the APS and drive the cultural change required for agencies to get more value out of the data they hold, for the benefit of all Australians.
