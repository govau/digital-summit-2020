Megan James

Case Study

WIC

Megan James is the President of Women in IT and Communications (WIC). WIC's mission as a Canberra-based, non-profit organisation is to 'bring people together to champion women in Information and Communication'. In addition to Megan's volunteer work (AIIA, WIC and BCNA) she is the Director Public Sector ANZ for Dataminr. She is a highly experienced senior executive who has had to navigate running a not for profit in extraordinary times as well as her other volunteer work and starting a new job in the middle of the pandemic.

Leadership through extraordinary times

As President of WIC, Megan has had to lead the organisation through a very difficult time. As a membership organisation that ran regular events Megan had to change the format and adapt quickly to providing value to members, sponsors and partners. Megan was passionate about providing support to her community during lockdown and providing a space for people to connect and support. At the same time Megan lost a job and gained a job. She will talk through managing these times and how her leadership has lead WIC to become an even stronger organisation with a more diverse reach.

Whether charity work or her real job, Megan is known for her ability to engage connect and lead.

This presentation will touch on both personal and business aspects on how she leads, why she leads and her other areas of passion.
