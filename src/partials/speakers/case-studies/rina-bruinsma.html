Rina Bruinsma

Case Study

National Bushfire Recovery Agency

Rina is the Deputy Coordinator for the Strategy and Governance Division at the National Bushfire Recovery Agency (NBRA). She is responsible for the NBRA's strategy and corporate functions, Bushfire Recovery Policy, including oversight of the National Bushfire Recovery Fund and the development of a national recovery plan, and communications and engagement. She joined the NBRA from the Department of Finance. She has a strong policy background and extensive experience leading reform and transformation across the public sector. She was recently involved in the Review of the Australian Public Service. Rina has worked in various roles in policy development and implementation since 2006, including labour market policy and the delivery of programs supporting vulnerable people. She also has vast experience in developing and delivering grants frameworks and simplifying access to financial support for applicants. She has a Bachelor of Arts and a Masters of Public Relations, and is currently undertaking a PhD in Communication and Creative Arts.

National Bushfire Recovery Agency

Rina from the National Bushfire Recovery Agency will speak about the establishment of the Agency following the Black Summer bushfires. In response to the COVID-19 pandemic, the Agency took its bushfire recovery efforts online and used its unique position to create a presence that cuts through red-tape, promotes transparency, and directly connects people with the help they need. They continue to deliver support to bushfire-affected people and stay true to their mantra, that is, if they don't know the answer, they'll connect you with someone who does.
