Ernez Dhondy

Speaker

General Manager, Paper Giant

With 15 years of experience in the industry, Ernez has established himself as an experienced design leader. Over the course of his career, Ernez has integrated and established design practices in some of Australia's largest digital and full-service agencies. He has mentored emerging design talent and a new generation of design leaders. He has led large-scale digital strategy and transformation programs across the public, private and social sectors. He has a deep understanding of these sectors and is passionate about helping clients experiment with new ways of working to generate new types of value. Ernez has worked across continents and been an active contributor to the digital culture in Australia across his entire career.

Can we co-create our way out of this?

COVID-19 has changed the way government, communities, and organisations work. It is the greatest societal change we are likely to experience, and we need to equip our organisations to deal with it. The pandemic didn't come with a playbook, strategic framework, or case study to help us navigate our way through it.

But we do have a starting point. I believe co-design is the most useful tool we have to help decision-makers through this crisis. It is a deeply human, community-based approach that can help government transform the way it delivers products and services to better meet the evolving needs of citizens. We can use co-design to leverage the thoughts, feelings, and intuitions at the coal face of this crisis. It can solve many of the issues the government currently faces and only through true participatory collaboration can we forge the best pathway out of this crisis.

I have recently completed work on a co-design project aimed at understanding and reacting to the needs of Australians experiencing mental health and wellbeing difficulties during COVID-19. The project produced more than just products and service concepts. It allowed leaders to work in partnership with individuals to fundamentally shift the strategic direction of the organisation.
