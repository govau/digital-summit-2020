Prof. Mal Thatcher

On-demand

Queensland University of Technology (QUT)

Mal is Professor of Digital Practice in the QUT Graduate School of Business. He is a career technologist and has held senior executive leadership positions in the public-sector and the private sectors. He has over 35 years' experience leading and delivering business value through technology-led innovation and transformation. Between 2004 and 2017 he held CIO roles in public and private sector organisations including Queensland Health, eHealth Queensland, and the Mater Hospital Group. He holds an Honours Degree in Computer Science, a Masters' in Applied Science and a PhD in Digital Risk and Governance. He has also completed a certificate program in IT Leadership from Harvard University.

Digital governance – what it is and why it is critical to the future of digital government

Mal will focus on the importance of digital governance as agencies progress their digital transformation agendas. Governance controls are critical to address risks that emerge throughout the digital investment life cycle. Mal will describe contemporary principles of digital governance through the lens of digital investment life cycle risk, and provide practical advice regarding the key governance controls essential to successful digital investment. He will also proivide an overview of a case study on failed digital investment in the public sector in Queensland and speak to the key independent findings as they relate to a failure in governance.

According to a 2019 report from Forbes, 70% of digital transformation programs fail to meet their stated objectives. Understanding and dealing with transformation risk is critical to success, yet few organisations do it well, mostly because they do not understand the risks due to a lack of competency in digital projects and digital business. Digital governance is a key competency for senior manager and executives as we move towards digital government.
