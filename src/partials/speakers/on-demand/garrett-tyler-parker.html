How systems thinking can enhance your design thinking

The philosophies of design thinking and systems thinking are complementary and not mutually exclusive.

Design thinking refers to applying a designer's sensibility and methods to problem solving, no matter what the problem is. Systems thinking is a complementary framework that sees relationships, rather than things. It can help in identify 'patterns of change' and how we can adjust a system to cause the most effective change.

Systems thinking can enhance the design process. It provides a diagnostic toolkit to put the problem in its appropriate context. Applicable to user experience, customer experience, and human-centred design, systems thinking helps examine the problem more holistically and accurately before acting and jumping into conclusions.

Garrett will share 6 basic systems thinking tools you can apply immediately in a design thinking framework and a wider context. He will also share examples of applying some of these tools in the government and private sector.
