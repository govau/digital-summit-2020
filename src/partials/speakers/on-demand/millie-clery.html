Checking it twice: digital transformation of the naughty and nice list with the Department of Christmas Affairs

In the lead-up to Christmas 2019, the newly established Department of Christmas Affairs (DCA) quickly needed a user-centred website. With looming deadlines and budgetary pressure, the DCA embarked on a rapid design journey which saw the launch of www.christmasaffairs.com on 4 December 2019.

Designing and delivering the website was a rollercoaster of setbacks and successes, tragedies and triumphs, heartbreak, and happiness. But by following the DTA's Service Design and Delivery process, the DCA's vision was realised. www.christmasaffairs.com had over 250,000 unique visitors in the 4-week lead-up to Christmas. It was featured in ABC National News, the Canberra Times, Her Canberra, and over 100 syndicated news channels across the USA. It also blew up on social media.

While the DCA is not a real government department, the website is real and there are real learnings to share. This presentation provides practical advice on how to (and how not to) design a user-friendly website.

The presentation will appeal to product owners, UX designers, and content designers. Anyone working in website design and delivery will leave this presentation with some easy-to-implement tips and tricks to improve their own processes.
