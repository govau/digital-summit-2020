Zoe Rose

Workshop

UX Designer and Trainer, Synergy

Zoe works in user experience design, information architecture, content design, and educational technologies. Zoe's career has spanned the UK and Australia. She has worked for the BBC, Cambridge University Press, GOV.UK, PwC and Seek. She has presented widely on disability topics, including at UX Australia. Her interest in the relationship between disability and design has deep roots. She acquired a serious disability after a fall in 2018. This experience deepened her commitment to talking about disability and the benefits designing for disability can bring to people who build and maintain services as widely as possible, especially in government.

Solve for one, extend to many: a practical hypothetical exercise in inclusive design for people with disabilities

Disability is an under-valued element of human diversity that is insufficiently understood in technology. The principles and benefits of inclusive design are now well established. But how can they be applied in government? In this workshop, participants will undertake some structured activities before engaging in a collaborative design challenge. This workshop is hypothetical. We will engage with a persona rather than conduct user research with a person with a disability.
