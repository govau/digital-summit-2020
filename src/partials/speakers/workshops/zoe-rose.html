Zoe Rose

Workshop

UX Designer and Trainer, Synergy

Zoe works in user experience design and design training. Her career has spanned the UK and Australia, and she has worked for organisations like the BBC, Cambridge University Press, GOV.UK, PwC and Seek. Zoe's interest in the relationship between disability and design has deep roots. She has presented widely on disability topics, including at UX Australia. Zoe also acquired a significant disability after a fall in 2018, an experience that has deepened her commitment to helping people who build web services understand disability and the benefits that designing for disability can bring.

Solve for one, extend to many: designing for inclusion

The principles and benefits of inclusive design are now well established, but how can they be applied in government?

In this workshop, we will find ways to remove barriers faced by a hypothetical person with a disability in real scenarios. You will find out how embracing the complexities and constraints that disability introduces can lead to products and services that are better for everyone.

We will explore the fundamental principles of inclusive design, the prevalence of different forms of disability, and the main models of disability. We will not cover technical aspects of accessibility. No technical knowledge is required or assumed for this workshop.

This workshop is for anyone who is involved in creating and delivering services, on or off the web.
