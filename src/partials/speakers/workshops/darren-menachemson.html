55 minutes in the digital ethics pressure cooker

It's 2025. Sustainability is at the top of the world's agenda, and Australia is conducting a bold policy experiment that rewards households consuming sustainably, through the tax system.

You will assume the role of a Digital Ethics Analyst, and work with a small team of peers to reflect on a particularly controversial aspect of the policy design. Your job is to confront a difficult digital ethics dilemma and think through the digital ethics implications.

We will introduce you to important ethics concepts and a digital ethics framework that can guide your thinking. But make no mistake: it will not be clear cut, and it will not feel comfortable.

The course is suited to those currently or potentially involved in digital transformation programs, with a decision-making, design or assurance role. You do not need prior knowledge of digital ethics or ethics theory.

This will debut a new workshop from ThinkPlace. Thinkplace have a strong track record of running inspiring and energising Pressure Cooker sessions where participants take on a realistic digital or policy ethics challenge.
