Jane Cipants

Workshop

Director, Client Service, Legal Aid NSW

Jane has spent over 20 years in the legal assistance sector in management, service design and delivery. Previously she was a social worker in the mental health sector. Jane holds a Bachelor of Social Work and Masters' degrees in social policy and public administration.

Digital design and disadvantaged clients: bringing voices at the margins to the centre of digital service design and delivery

Our Legal Aid NSW Strategic Plan places clients at the centre of everything we do. Through our digital work program, we co-designed the development of digital tools with highly disadvantaged clients who considered themselves digitally excluded and had challenges engaging with government services. We brought their voice to every step of the design process to improve their experience of getting help.

Our clients are often marginalised. Being part of these projects empowered them and made sure their voices were heard and needs met. For many, it was the first time an 'institution' had asked their opinion and encouraged them to share the expertise of their lived experience.

The global COVID-19 pandemic did not prevent us from engaging and designing with our clients. We share how to recruit and engage with clients online in a virtual context to maximise their input and engagement.
